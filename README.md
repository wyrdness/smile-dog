## Overview

**Smile Dog** is a creepypasta (internet horror story) that originated around 2008 on 4chan's /x/ (paranormal) board. The fictional narrative describes a supposedly "cursed image" called "smile.jpg" depicting a Siberian Husky with an unnaturally wide, human-like smile in a dark room.

According to the **fictional story**:
- Viewing the image causes nightmares and psychological distress
- Victims are compelled to "spread the word" by showing it to others (chain letter mechanic)
- The dog appears in dreams demanding the image be shared
- Only by spreading it can one be free of the curse

**Reality**: This is creative horror fiction. There is no actual cursed image. Smile Dog represents an important cultural phenomenon in early internet horror storytelling and the development of "creepypasta" as a genre.

### Learn More About Creepypasta

**What is Creepypasta?**
- Short horror stories shared on internet forums
- Often presented as "true" for narrative effect
- Genre emerged ~2007 on 4chan and similar boards

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Smile Dog real?

**No.** Smile Dog is a fictional horror story created for entertainment. There is no actual cursed image, and viewing any image will not cause supernatural effects.

### Is there a "real" smile.jpg?

No single canonical image exists. Various images have been associated with the story over the years, but they are either created for the story or misattributed to it. None have supernatural properties.

### Why do people share it if it's fake?

Because it's an entertaining horror story. People enjoy sharing scary stories, especially in online horror communities. The "spread the word" element is part of the story's charm, mimicking chain letter mechanics.

### Is it safe to view images associated with the story?

Yes. Any images associated with Smile Dog are normal photographs with no supernatural properties. However, they may be intentionally disturbing in content (unsettling imagery designed to be creepy).

### Who created Smile Dog?

The original author is unknown or anonymous, typical of early creepypasta. It emerged from 4chan's /x/ board around 2008.
